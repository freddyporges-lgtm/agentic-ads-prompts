# Campaign Analysis Prompts

---

## 1. Performance Variance Diagnosis

Use when a campaign is over- or under-delivering and you need to identify the likely cause before a stakeholder meeting.

```
I'm analyzing a campaign that is [over/under] delivering against goal.

Here is the performance data:
- Campaign goal: [awareness / clicks / conversions / ROAS]
- Planned delivery: [impressions / spend / clicks]
- Actual delivery: [impressions / spend / clicks]
- Time period: [date range]
- Key metrics: CTR [x%], CVR [x%], CPM [x], CPC [x]

Targeting setup:
- Audience: [description]
- Placements: [channels / sites / formats]
- Geo: [targeting]
- Dayparting: [if any]

Diagnose the most likely causes of the variance. For each cause, explain the signal in the data that points to it and suggest a specific action to test. Prioritize by what is most likely to have the biggest impact if addressed.
```

## 2. Stakeholder Performance Summary

Use when you need to translate raw campaign data into a clear, non-technical summary for a marketing or business stakeholder.

```
I need to write a performance summary for a non-technical stakeholder.

Campaign context:
- Advertiser / brand: [name]
- Campaign objective: [what success looks like for them]
- Flight dates: [start - end]
- Budget: [total / spent to date]

Performance data:
[paste your metrics here - impressions, clicks, conversions, spend, ROAS, etc.]

Write a 3-4 paragraph summary that:
1. Opens with the headline result (is this working or not?)
2. Explains the 2-3 metrics that matter most for this objective and what they mean
3. Calls out anything that needs attention or a decision
4. Ends with a clear recommendation for what to do next

Write it for someone who understands their business but does not think in CPMs or CTRs.
```

## 3. Pacing Analysis

Use when a campaign is mis-pacing and you need to understand whether to adjust bids, budget, or targeting.

```
A campaign is [ahead / behind] pace with [X] days remaining in the flight.

Current pacing:
- Total budget: [amount]
- Spent to date: [amount]
- Expected spend at this point: [amount]
- Days elapsed: [X] of [Y] total days
- Daily cap: [if applicable]

Auction and delivery context:
- Bid strategy: [CPM / CPC / CPA / tROAS]
- Current bid: [amount]
- Win rate: [if known]
- Impression share: [if known]

Given this pacing situation, walk me through:
1. The most likely root cause
2. The levers available to correct it (bid, budget, targeting, creative, schedule)
3. The tradeoffs of each lever
4. Your recommended action and why
```

## 4. Post-Campaign Readout

Use to structure a full post-campaign analysis that captures learnings, not just results.

```
I'm writing a post-campaign readout. Help me structure the analysis and identify the most important learnings.

Campaign details:
- Objective: [what we were trying to achieve]
- Hypothesis going in: [what we expected to work and why]
- Flight: [dates, budget, channels]

Results:
[paste performance data]

Audience / targeting used:
[description]

Creative / messaging:
[description of what ran]

Given this, help me:
1. Assess whether the campaign achieved its objective and by how much
2. Identify what worked and what did not, with evidence from the data
3. Surface 2-3 specific learnings that should change how we approach the next campaign
4. Flag any questions the data raises that we cannot answer from what we have

Format as a structured readout I can share with the team.
```
